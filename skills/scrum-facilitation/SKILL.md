# Scrum Facilitation

## When to Use
- Preparing or facilitating sprint planning, standups, reviews, or retrospectives.
- Improving backlog readiness, sprint focus, or action follow-through.
- Coaching teams and stakeholders on agile collaboration.
- Resolving ceremony fatigue or low-value meeting patterns.

## Outcomes
- Ceremonies that create clarity, alignment, and useful decisions.
- Better sprint focus, healthier flow, and clearer team commitments.
- More actionable retrospectives with visible follow-through.
- Improved collaboration between team, product, and stakeholders.

## Core Principles
1. Facilitate the team toward ownership rather than dictating decisions.
2. Use ceremony time to create clarity and action, not status theater.
3. Protect focus, timeboxes, and psychological safety consistently.
4. Ground discussions in real work, data, and observable constraints.
5. Close loops by reviewing previous commitments and improvement actions.

## Standard Workflow
1. Clarify the purpose, desired outcome, participants, and inputs for the ceremony.
2. Prepare agenda, board state, metrics, and facilitation prompts in advance.
3. Run the session with strong timeboxing, inclusive participation, and visible notes.
4. Convert insights into concrete decisions, owners, or action items.
5. Review outcomes in the next cycle and adjust the format if it is not delivering value.

## Checklist — Do
- Use planning to align on goal, capacity, and dependencies rather than just filling a sprint.
- Keep standups focused on coordination, blockers, and sprint movement.
- Facilitate retrospectives to produce one or two realistic improvements, not a long wish list.
- Use metrics like burndown, cycle time, or carryover as inputs, not weapons.
- Create safety so people can surface risk, conflict, and uncertainty early.

## Checklist — Avoid
- Avoid turning standups into status reporting to management.
- Avoid overcommitting during planning to satisfy external pressure.
- Avoid retrospectives that collect feedback but never revisit actions.
- Avoid blaming individuals for system or process issues.
- Avoid ceremony rituals that the team cannot explain the value of.

## Example Prompts
- "Help me facilitate a sprint planning session for this backlog."
- "Design a retrospective format for a team with low energy and high carryover."
- "Create a standup structure that surfaces blockers without wasting time."
- "Coach us on improving our sprint review with stakeholders."

## Deliverables
- Ceremony agenda and facilitation guidance.
- Action-item and follow-through recommendations.
- Metrics to support the conversation responsibly.
- Improvement suggestions for the team operating model.
